Hikkaduwa Hatchery

The Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Centre is located a few kilometers north of the main Hikkaduwa town, with the goal of protecting some of the world's endangered turtle species.

What to See

The Turtle Hatchery, located near Hikkaduwa, is a private research organization dedicated to rescuing endangered marine turtles. It showcases turtle eggs, demonstrates turtle development, and offers the opportunity to release newborn turtles into the ocean for an additional cost. The recommended time to visit and witness the turtles is during March to April. Visitors can encounter sightings mentioned below.

  • Turtles lay their eggs in the centre.
  • The sea turtle hatchery and rescue center's water tanks to care for young turtles.
  • Adult turtle species retrieved from the water as a result of injuries.

More About Hikkaduwa Hatchery

The admission cost is approximately LKR 500, although donations are appreciated. The sea turtle is a critically endangered species. The primary cause of this is human activities. There is a thriving criminal trade in turtle eggs, shells, and meat. Turtles are also being harmed by boats and fishing nets. Increased ocean pollution is another element that is detrimental to turtles.

Importance

This center is dedicated to preserving endangered turtle species and is among several turtle hatcheries along the southern coast. Originally established as a community initiative to safeguard biodiversity in the region, it has evolved into a comprehensive hatchery and rescue facility. The center now operates with committed teams working tirelessly to research, nurture, and safeguard various turtle species.
