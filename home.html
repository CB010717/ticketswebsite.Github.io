Sea turtles play a crucial role within marine ecosystems. Turtles hold significant cultural importance and are highly valued for tourism. Five out of the seven turtle species inhabit various regions globally, primarily in tropical and subtropical waters.



A glimpse to the world of turtles.

Turtles spend the most of their lives in the water. They are aquatic-adapted, possessing webbed feet or flippers and a streamlined body.

There are distinct differences between them.

  1. Except to lay eggs in the beach, sea turtles rarely leave the ocean.
  2. Freshwater turtles reside in ponds and lakes, and they emerge from the water to sunbathe in the sun on logs or rocks.
  3. Tortoises are terrestrial animals with round and stubby feet that are well-suited for walking on land
  4. Terrapins can be found both on land and in water, most commonly in swamps, ponds, lakes, and rivers.

Sea turtles

Sea turtles, which are large reptiles that rely on air for breathing, can be found in tropical and subtropical oceans across the globe.

How to determine Sea Turtles

  1. Shells consists of an upper part which is called "carapace".
  2. They also have a lower section called "plastron".
  3. With the exception of the leatherback, the bodies of sea turtles are protected by tough scales, known as scutes.
  4. The species of sea turtles can be identified by analyzing the quantity and pattern of their scales.


Fun Facts about Sea Turtles

Do you know

  • Green sea turtles are the only marine turtles that are predominantly herbivores.
  • Sea turtles lay eggs in nests they dig in the sand.
  • Temperature in nest determines sex of sea turtles.
  • Hawksbill turtles retrieve their favorite prey with their beaks.
  • The only marine turtles that nest during the day are Kemp's ridleys.
  • Unlike other turtles ,Sea turtles do not retract into their shells.

Are sea turtles endangered ?

Humans are the greatest threat to sea turtles, not natural predators.
