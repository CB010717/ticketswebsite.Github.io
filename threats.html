Threats


Six of the world's seven sea turtle species are endangered or threatened owing to poaching, being trapped in fishing gear, plastic pollution, and a variety of other factors. As responsible humans we should support critical turtle nesting beaches all around the world when human activities such as egg consumption endanger sea turtle populations.

Plastic and Sea Turtles

Sea turtles are heavily impacted by global plastic pollution, facing numerous challenges throughout their life cycle. From encountering plastic-infested waters and beaches during nesting to ingesting toxic microplastics and mistaking plastic for food, they are vulnerable to various plastic-related dangers. They can also suffer from injuries caused by plastic debris such as straws, spoons, six-pack rings, and ghost fishing equipment. The detrimental effects of plastic on sea turtles are widespread and unavoidable.

Illegal trade of turtles

Hawksbill sea turtles are a visually stunning and vital species in the ocean. Their role in maintaining the health of coral reefs by consuming sponges is significant, and their presence in the wild is highly appreciated by divers and snorkelers. However, their population has been drastically reduced due to excessive hunting driven by the demand for their beautiful shells, also known as "tortoiseshell." These shells are often crafted into jewelry and other items, which are commonly sold to tourists in markets across Latin America, Asia, and other regions

Climate change

Sea turtles are facing increasingly severe risks due to the impacts of climate change. The rising sea levels and intensified storms pose a significant threat to their beach habitats, leading to erosion and destruction. Additionally, the warming of oceans is altering ocean currents, which could expose sea turtles to new predators and negatively affect the coral reefs essential for their survival.
